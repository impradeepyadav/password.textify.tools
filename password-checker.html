What Makes a Strong Password?
A strong password is at least 16 characters long, uses a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols, does not contain dictionary words or personal information, and is not reused across multiple accounts. Our checker evaluates your password against these criteria and more.

Password Strength Criteria Explained

  • Length (40% of score): Longer passwords are exponentially stronger. Aim for 16+ characters.
  • Character Variety (20% of score): Mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols increases complexity.
  • Uniqueness (20% of score): Not in common password lists or dictionaries.
  • Pattern Avoidance (20% of score): No keyboard patterns, sequential numbers, or repeated characters.

Common Password Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using personal information: Birthdays, pet names, anniversaries are easily found on social media
  • Keyboard patterns: "qwerty123", "asdfgh", "1qaz2wsx" - first combinations hackers try
  • Common substitutions: "P@ssw0rd" - hackers have dictionaries of these patterns
  • Reusing passwords: One breach compromises all accounts using the same password
  • Short passwords: Under 12 characters can be cracked in hours with modern GPUs
  • Dictionary words: Any word in the dictionary reduces security significantly

How to Create a Strong Password

  1. Use sufficient length: Minimum 16 characters for strong security
  2. Mix character types: Include uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  3. Avoid patterns: No dictionary words, keyboard patterns, or personal information
  4. Use randomness: Let our password generator create truly random sequences
  5. Never reuse: Each account needs a unique password
  6. Use a password manager: Store generated passwords securely

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the strongest password?
The strongest passwords are randomly generated, at least 20 characters long, and use a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Our password generator creates such passwords with 100+ bits of entropy, making them mathematically unbreakable with current technology.
How long should my password be?
For most accounts, aim for 16-20 characters. Email and banking accounts should use 20+ characters. Use the maximum length allowed by each service. Each additional character exponentially increases password strength.
Are password managers safe to use?
Yes, password managers are highly recommended. They allow you to use unique, strong passwords for every account without memorizing them. Choose a reputable manager like Bitwarden, 1Password, or Dashlane with strong master password and 2FA.
How often should I change my passwords?
NIST no longer recommends periodic password changes unless there's evidence of compromise. Instead, change passwords when: a service reports a breach, you suspect unauthorized access, or you reuse a password across sites. Focus on creating strong, unique passwords initially.
