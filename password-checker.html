Why Standard Complexity Rules Fail

Many websites still rely on outdated rules like "minimum 8 characters, one uppercase, one number." Our checker goes far beyond these minimums because hackers have long since adapted. Here's why our advanced checks matter:

  • Common Password Detection: Even if a password like "P@ssw0rd2024!" passes basic complexity filters, it's virtually useless. Hackers use massive databases of breached passwords to try millions of combinations in seconds.
  • Keyboard Walk Detection: Patterns like "qwerty", "1qaz2wsx", or "zaq12wsx" are visually clever but are part of standard brute-force dictionaries. Our tool flags these immediately.
  • Sequential Number Detection: Using "123" or "2026" at the end of a password adds almost zero security against a targeted attack.

What Makes a Strong Password?
A strong password is at least 16 characters long, uses a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols, does not contain dictionary words or personal information, and is not reused across multiple accounts. Our checker evaluates your password against these criteria and more.

Password Strength Criteria Explained

  • Length (40% of score): Longer passwords are exponentially stronger. Aim for 16+ characters.
  • Character Variety (20% of score): Mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols increases complexity.
  • Uniqueness (20% of score): Not in common password lists or dictionaries.
  • Pattern Avoidance (20% of score): No keyboard patterns, sequential numbers, or repeated characters.

A Better Way: The Passphrase Method

If you find it impossible to remember a complex string like J9$kL2@pQw, you are not alone. Modern security guidelines recommend **passphrases**. A passphrase is a string of random words.

Example: Correct-Horse-Battery-Staple
This password uses only lowercase letters and dashes, but its length (28 characters) gives it enormous entropy. It's mathematically much stronger than P@ssw0rd and infinitely easier to remember.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the strongest password?
The strongest passwords are randomly generated, at least 20 characters long, and use a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Our password generator creates such passwords with 100+ bits of entropy, making them mathematically unbreakable with current technology.
How long should my password be?
For most accounts, aim for 16-20 characters. Email and banking accounts should use 20+ characters. Use the maximum length allowed by each service. Each additional character exponentially increases password strength.
