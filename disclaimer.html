4. Security Disclaimer: Understanding Risks

No security tool can guarantee 100% protection. Please understand these known limitations:

Known Limitations

  • Browser Security: If your browser is compromised by malware, generated passwords could be visible to attackers
  • Device Malware: Keyloggers, screen capture malware, or other malware on your device could steal passwords
  • Network Sniffing: While we use HTTPS, compromised networks or SSL stripping attacks pose risks
  • Physical Access: Someone with direct access to your device could see generated passwords on screen
  • Future Technology: Quantum computing may eventually weaken current cryptographic standards
  • Social Engineering: Even strong passwords can be phished if you're tricked into revealing them

Your Responsibilities

  • Keep your devices, browsers, and operating systems updated
  • Use reputable antivirus and anti-malware software
  • Be aware of your surroundings when generating passwords in public
  • Store passwords in encrypted password managers (never in plain text)
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) wherever possible
  • Never share passwords via email, SMS, or unencrypted channels
  • Use unique passwords for every account
