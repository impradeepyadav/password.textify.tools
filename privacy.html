Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 2026 | Last Updated: March 2026

🔐 Zero-Knowledge Architecture: At Password Generator, we take a radical approach to privacy. If we don't collect it, we can't lose it, leak it, or be compelled to hand it over. Every password you generate exists only in your browser's temporary memory and is erased the moment you close the tab.
📢 Google AdSense Compliance: This website may display advertisements through Google AdSense. Google uses cookies to serve ads based on your prior visits to our website or other websites. You may opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Google Ad Settings. We do not share any personally identifiable information with advertisers.

Our Privacy Promise to You

We built Password Generator because we were tired of seeing "secure" password tools that actually sent your data to servers for "analysis" or "storage." We make the following ironclad guarantees:

  • We Never See Your Passwords: The generation happens in JavaScript running on your device. Our servers never receive your password. You can verify this by opening your browser's Developer Tools (F12), going to the Network tab, and clicking "Generate." You will see exactly zero network requests containing your password.
  • We Don't Use Tracking Cookies: Unlike most websites, we do not use Facebook Pixel, Google Analytics, or any cross-site tracking scripts. The only cookies present are essential session cookies from Google AdSense (if ads are displayed).
  • We Don't Want Your Email: You don't need to create an account or sign up for a newsletter. You can use this tool forever without telling us who you are.
  • Open Source Verification: While our business logic is proprietary, the cryptographic methods we use (window.crypto.getRandomValues) are open standards vetted by security researchers worldwide.

1. No Data Collection

The passwords generated by this tool are created using the window.crypto.getRandomValues() API. This process happens entirely within your device's local memory. We do not store, log, or transmit any generated passwords. We do not maintain any database of user-generated content. There is no "history" feature because we believe that storing passwords is the job of a dedicated password manager, not a website.

2. Client-Side Processing

All password generation occurs in your browser. Your data never leaves your device. This means:

  • No servers involved in password creation
  • No database of user information
  • No tracking of generated passwords
  • No third-party access to your keys
  • No way for us to see what you generate
  • Complete functionality even when offline after initial page load

Proof of Concept: Once this page is open, turn off your Wi-Fi. Click "Generate New Password." It still works. That is the proof that the data never leaves your computer.

Technical Deep Dive: How We Stay Blind

Our architecture is deliberately simple to maximize privacy:

  • Static HTML/CSS/JavaScript: The website is a collection of static files. There is no backend server processing user input. This eliminates the attack surface of server-side code injection or database leaks.
  • Web Crypto API: We leverage the native cryptographic library built into modern browsers. This library is maintained by browser vendors (Google, Mozilla, Apple) and is constantly audited for security flaws.
  • Content Security Policy (CSP): We use strict CSP headers to prevent any unauthorized scripts from running on our pages, protecting you from supply-chain attacks.

4. Your Rights Under GDPR and CCPA

Since we don't collect personal data, compliance is built-in. You have the right to access, rectify, and erase data — but we have none to provide. To be explicit:

  • Right to Access (GDPR Art. 15): We have no personal data to show you.
  • Right to Erasure (GDPR Art. 17): There is no data to delete.
  • Right to Data Portability (GDPR Art. 20): You already have the only copy (the password on your screen).
  • CCPA "Do Not Sell My Info": We don't have any of your info to sell.

If you are a resident of California, Colorado, Connecticut, Utah, or Virginia, you have specific rights regarding your personal information. As we collect no personal information, there is nothing to disclose or delete.

5. Children's Privacy (COPPA Compliance)

Password Generator does not knowingly collect any Personal Identifiable Information from children under the age of 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information (which, by our architecture, is impossible, but we include this for legal clarity), please contact us. Since we do not collect data, there is no data to remove.

We advise you to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes. Changes to this Privacy Policy are effective when they are posted on this page.
